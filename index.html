The Intelligent Offer Engine: Maximizing CLV and Margin Efficiency (TXU Energy)

Focus: Revenue Growth Management, Advanced Predictive Modeling, Data Architecture

Challenge: Operating within a highly competitive market, generic, expensive retention offers were severely eroding margins and driving high customer turnover. The imperative was to replace this blanket approach with a data-driven, margin-aware strategy for hyper-personalized retention and pricing.

Solution: I personally architected and led the development of the Intelligent Offer Engine (IOE), transforming it into our internal Revenue Growth Management platform. The effort started with a foundational shift in data engineering: centralizing fragmented customer, billing, and interaction data into a high-performance Snowflake feature store. Using complex, optimized SQL Stored Procedures, I successfully productionized over 40 behavioral and transactional features critical for customer profiling. The IOE's technical core was a dual-score predictive engine built in Python, leveraging Advanced Classification Models to calculate the exact churn risk, while a parallel statistical model forecasted profitability robustly against market volatility. I meticulously aligned hyperparameter tuning with commercial outcomes (margin erosion minimization) and engineered the final optimization layer to dynamically sequence targeted offers, ensuring every action maximized profit while improving product fit. This complex model was translated into clear, A/B testing-validated financial outcomes for senior leadership, establishing a clear roadmap for accretive growth.

Quantified Impact (Profit & Retention):

The deployment of the IOE delivered targeted renewal hierarchies that secured the highest loss rate benefit in the most price-sensitive segments, successfully reducing overall portfolio attrition by 10% and simultaneously increasing Customer Lifetime Value by 6%.

High-Value Customer Segmentation: Driving Profitable Renewable Adoption (TXU Energy)

Focus: Unsupervised Machine Learning, Targeted Marketing Strategy, Resource Allocation

Challenge: Inefficient, broad marketing prevented the company from successfully capitalizing on its high-margin renewable energy plans. The challenge was to move beyond simple demographics to identify non-obvious, high-propensity segments for green energy adoption, ensuring marketing spend was highly profitable.

Solution: Taking the strategic lead, I built a comprehensive, multi-dimensional feature set by integrating customer demographics, full purchase history, and detailed behavioral data, recognizing that traditional segmentation was inadequate. I selected and implemented an Unsupervised Clustering Model, leveraging its ability to capture latent preference patterns that rule-based systems would miss. I rigorously used statistical methods (like the Elbow method) for determining the optimal number of clusters and aligned the entire tuning process with projected business metrics to maximize economic outcomes. The model’s output was translated into actionable strategy: I identified and named the most valuable group the 'Green Adopters' segment and presented clear financial projections to executive leadership, securing approval for a profitable reallocation of resources to a dedicated, high-exposure digital campaign.

Quantified Impact (Revenue Growth):

The strategic redirection of marketing resources to the highly targeted 'Green Adopters' campaign achieved a direct and measurable 15% increase in renewable plan adoption, establishing a profitable pathway for new revenue streams.
